										<h2><a href="#">Taiwan stocks rise</a></h2>
										<p>
											This rise was seen after the historic meeting between Ma Ying-jeou and Xi Jinping
										</p>
									</header>
									<a href="#" class="image featured"><img src="../images/Taiwan.jpg" height=300px alt="" /></a>
									<p><b>TAIPEI:</b> Taiwan stocks rose on Monday after leaders of Taiwan and China met for the first time in more than six decades at the weekend, led by gains in heavyweights such as TSMC. </p>
									<p>As of 1:31 GMT, the main TAIEX index rose 0.3 per cent, to 8,715.02 points, after closing at 8,693.57 points in the previous session. </p>
									<p>The electronics subindex rose 0.3 per cent, while the financials subindex gained 0.4 per cent. </p>
									<p>Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co (TSMC), the world's top contract chip maker, added 1.8 per cent. Mediatek, which is Taiwan's biggest chip design house and a major player in the China market, rose 0.5 per cent. 

The Taiwan dollar firmed T$0.085 to T$32.645 per US dollar. </p>
